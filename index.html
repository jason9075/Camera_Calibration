Camera Calibration — Math Primer

1. Pinhole Camera Model

A 3-D world point $\mathbf{X} = (X, Y, Z)^\top$ projects to image pixel $(u, v)$ via:

$$\begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \sim K \begin{bmatrix} R \mid t \end{bmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} X \\ Y \\ Z \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

The intrinsic matrix $K$ encodes focal length and principal point:

$$K = \begin{pmatrix} f_x & 0 & c_x \\ 0 & f_y & c_y \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

This simulation assumes square pixels ($f_x = f_y = f$) and a centred principal point ($c_x = W/2,\; c_y = H/2$), so there is only one unknown: the focal length $f$.

2. Homography — Board Plane to Image

Start from the full projection. The extrinsic matrix $[R \mid t]$ is $3 \times 4$, with $R = [r_1 \mid r_2 \mid r_3]$:

$$\tilde{\mathbf{u}} \sim K\,[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid r_3 \mid t]\begin{pmatrix}X\\Y\\Z\\1\end{pmatrix}$$

The calibration board lies entirely on the plane $Z = 0$, so every world point satisfies $Z = 0$. Substituting eliminates the $r_3 Z$ term entirely:

$$\tilde{\mathbf{u}} \sim K\,[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid r_3 \mid t]\begin{pmatrix}X\\Y\\0\\1\end{pmatrix} = K\,[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid t]\begin{pmatrix}X\\Y\\1\end{pmatrix}$$

The $3 \times 4$ extrinsic collapses to a $3 \times 3$ matrix. Absorbing $K$ yields the homography:

$$\boxed{H = K\bigl[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid t\bigr]}$$

Each captured view contributes one homography $H$ with 8 degrees of freedom. At least 4 non-collinear corners per view are required.

3. DLT — Computing H via Least Squares

For each corner correspondence $(X_i, Y_i) \leftrightarrow (u_i, v_i)$, the cross-product constraint $\tilde{\mathbf{u}} \times H\tilde{\mathbf{X}} = 0$ yields two linear equations:

$$\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{X}_i^\top & \mathbf{0} & -u_i\,\mathbf{X}_i^\top \\ \mathbf{0} & \mathbf{X}_i^\top & -v_i\,\mathbf{X}_i^\top \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{h} = \mathbf{0}$$

Stack $n$ corners into matrix $A$, fix $h_9 = 1$, and solve the $8 \times 8$ normal equations $A^\top A\,\mathbf{h} = A^\top \mathbf{b}$ via Gaussian elimination with partial pivoting.

Hartley normalisation — both point sets are translated to the origin and scaled so the mean point-to-origin distance is $\sqrt{2}$, dramatically improving the numerical conditioning of $A^\top A$.

4. Zhang's Constraints — Recovering $f$

Rotation columns are orthonormal: $r_1 \cdot r_2 = 0$ and $\lVert r_1 \rVert = \lVert r_2 \rVert$. Substituting $r_i = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_i$ and letting $\mathbf{p}_i = K^{-1}h_i$ (centred) gives two independent $f^2$ estimates per view:

$$f^2 = \frac{-(p_{1x}p_{2x}+p_{1y}p_{2y})}{p_{1z}p_{2z}} \qquad \text{(orthogonality: }r_1\cdot r_2=0\text{)}$$ $$f^2 = \frac{p_{2x}^2+p_{2y}^2-p_{1x}^2-p_{1y}^2}{p_{1z}^2-p_{2z}^2} \qquad \text{(equal norm: }\lVert r_1\rVert=\lVert r_2\rVert\text{)}$$

Only estimates with $f^2 > 0$ are kept. The median across all views and both constraints is taken as the final focal length.

Ground truth check: $f_y = \dfrac{H/2}{\tan(\mathrm{FOV}/2)}$ — the focal length is directly derivable from the Three.js perspective FOV.

5. Pose Recovery (R, t per View)

With $f$ known, the scale factor $\lambda = \lVert K^{-1}h_1 \rVert$ normalises each homography column to unit length:

$$r_1 = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_1, \quad r_2 = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_2, \quad r_3 = r_1 \times r_2, \quad t = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_3$$

$r_3$ is recovered via the cross product, ensuring a right-handed coordinate frame.

6. RMS Reprojection Error

Each 3-D object point is projected through the estimated $(K, R, t)$ and compared with the measured pixel:

$$\begin{pmatrix} X_c \\ Y_c \\ Z_c \end{pmatrix} = R\mathbf{X}+t, \qquad \hat{u} = f\frac{X_c}{Z_c}+c_x, \quad \hat{v} = f\frac{Y_c}{Z_c}+c_y$$ $$\mathrm{RMS} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{NM}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{j=1}^{M}\bigl\lVert\hat{\mathbf{p}}_{ij}-\mathbf{p}_{ij}\bigr\rVert^2}$$

$N$ = views, $M$ = corners per view. Below $0.5\,\mathrm{px}$ → Excellent  |  $0.5$–$1.5\,\mathrm{px}$ → Acceptable  |  above $1.5\,\mathrm{px}$ → Too large. With Gaussian noise $\sigma$, the theoretical minimum RMS approaches $\sigma$ as the number of views grows.

7. Board Conventions — Unwritten Rules

Asymmetric grid (one odd, one even): Always use a grid where the two corner counts differ — e.g. $9 \times 6$ or $7 \times 8$, never $8 \times 8$. A symmetric board has $180°$ rotational symmetry, so the detector cannot distinguish the board's orientation and the recovered $R$ may have random axis flips.

Object-space coordinate system (the frame passed to calibrateCamera):

  • Origin $(0,0,0)$ — top-left inner corner (first detected point, red dot in this demo).
  • $X$-axis (red arrow) — points right, advancing along columns (long edge).
  • $Y$-axis (green arrow) — points down, advancing along rows (short edge).
  • $Z$-axis — by the right-hand rule, points away from the camera (into the board).

Row-major scan order: The object-point list walks columns first ($X$), then rows ($Y$), matching the detector output:

$$\mathrm{idx} = r \cdot \mathrm{cols} + c, \qquad \mathbf{X}_{rc} = (c \cdot s,\; r \cdot s,\; 0)$$

where $s$ is the physical square size (e.g. metres).

Senior tips — (1) Use real physical units for $s$: the recovered $t$ then has metric meaning ($t_z = 0.5$ → camera is 50 cm from the board). (2) Leave at least one square of white border (quiet zone) around the grid; missing contrast breaks corner detection at the edges. (3) Never use a square grid ($N \times N$): a $90°$ rotation silently swaps $X$ and $Y$, destabilising pose estimates in SLAM or multi-camera rigs.

1. 針孔相機模型

三維世界座標 $\mathbf{X} = (X, Y, Z)^\top$ 透過下式投影至像素座標 $(u, v)$：

$$\begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \sim K \begin{bmatrix} R \mid t \end{bmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} X \\ Y \\ Z \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

內參矩陣 $K$ 包含焦距與主點資訊：

$$K = \begin{pmatrix} f_x & 0 & c_x \\ 0 & f_y & c_y \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

本系統假設方形像素（$f_x = f_y = f$）且主點置中（$c_x = W/2,\; c_y = H/2$），因此只剩一個未知數：焦距 $f$。

2. 單應矩陣 — 標定板平面到影像

從完整投影公式出發。外參矩陣 $[R \mid t]$ 為 $3 \times 4$，其中 $R = [r_1 \mid r_2 \mid r_3]$：

$$\tilde{\mathbf{u}} \sim K\,[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid r_3 \mid t]\begin{pmatrix}X\\Y\\Z\\1\end{pmatrix}$$

標定板完全位於 $Z = 0$ 平面，所有世界座標點均滿足 $Z = 0$。代入後，$r_3 Z$ 項完全消去：

$$\tilde{\mathbf{u}} \sim K\,[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid r_3 \mid t]\begin{pmatrix}X\\Y\\0\\1\end{pmatrix} = K\,[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid t]\begin{pmatrix}X\\Y\\1\end{pmatrix}$$

$3 \times 4$ 的外參矩陣因此退化為 $3 \times 3$。再乘上內參 $K$ 即得單應矩陣：

$$\boxed{H = K\bigl[r_1 \mid r_2 \mid t\bigr]}$$

每個拍攝視角提供一個具有 8 個自由度的單應矩陣 $H$。每個視角至少需要 4 個不共線的角點才能求解。

3. DLT — 最小二乘法計算單應矩陣

對每個角點對應 $(X_i, Y_i) \leftrightarrow (u_i, v_i)$，叉積約束 $\tilde{\mathbf{u}} \times H\tilde{\mathbf{X}} = 0$ 展開為兩條線性方程：

$$\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{X}_i^\top & \mathbf{0} & -u_i\,\mathbf{X}_i^\top \\ \mathbf{0} & \mathbf{X}_i^\top & -v_i\,\mathbf{X}_i^\top \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{h} = \mathbf{0}$$

將 $n$ 個角點堆疊為矩陣 $A$，固定 $h_9 = 1$ 後，以部分主元高斯消去法求解 $8 \times 8$ 法方程 $A^\top A\,\mathbf{h} = A^\top \mathbf{b}$。

Hartley 正規化 — 兩組點集平移至原點並縮放，使平均點到原點距離為 $\sqrt{2}$，大幅改善 $A^\top A$ 的數值條件數，提升求解穩定性。

4. Zhang 的約束條件 — 還原焦距 $f$

旋轉矩陣具有正交歸一性：$r_1 \cdot r_2 = 0$ 且 $\lVert r_1 \rVert = \lVert r_2 \rVert$。代入 $r_i = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_i$，令 $\mathbf{p}_i = K^{-1}h_i$（中心平移後），每個視角可得兩個獨立的 $f^2$ 估計：

$$f^2 = \frac{-(p_{1x}p_{2x}+p_{1y}p_{2y})}{p_{1z}p_{2z}} \qquad \text{（正交性：}r_1\cdot r_2=0\text{）}$$ $$f^2 = \frac{p_{2x}^2+p_{2y}^2-p_{1x}^2-p_{1y}^2}{p_{1z}^2-p_{2z}^2} \qquad \text{（等模長：}\lVert r_1\rVert=\lVert r_2\rVert\text{）}$$

僅保留 $f^2 > 0$ 的有效估計，取所有視角與兩個約束的中位數作為最終焦距。

理論真值驗算：$f_y = \dfrac{H/2}{\tan(\mathrm{FOV}/2)}$，可直接由 Three.js 的透視 FOV 推導，用以評估校正精度。

5. 外參還原（每個視角的 R、t）

已知 $f$ 後，尺度因子 $\lambda = \lVert K^{-1}h_1 \rVert$ 將單應矩陣各列正規化為單位長度：

$$r_1 = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_1, \quad r_2 = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_2, \quad r_3 = r_1 \times r_2, \quad t = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}K^{-1}h_3$$

$r_3$ 透過叉積計算，確保構成右手座標系。

6. RMS 重投影誤差

將每個三維物體點透過估計的 $(K, R, t)$ 投影，與量測到的像素座標比較：

$$\begin{pmatrix} X_c \\ Y_c \\ Z_c \end{pmatrix} = R\mathbf{X}+t, \qquad \hat{u} = f\frac{X_c}{Z_c}+c_x, \quad \hat{v} = f\frac{Y_c}{Z_c}+c_y$$ $$\mathrm{RMS} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{NM}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{j=1}^{M}\bigl\lVert\hat{\mathbf{p}}_{ij}-\mathbf{p}_{ij}\bigr\rVert^2}$$

$N$ 為視角數，$M$ 為每張的角點數。 低於 $0.5\,\mathrm{px}$ → 優秀 |  $0.5$–$1.5\,\mathrm{px}$ → 可接受 |  超過 $1.5\,\mathrm{px}$ → 過大。 在高斯噪聲 $\sigma$ 下，隨視角數增加，RMS 理論下限趨近於 $\sigma$。

7. 標定板慣例 — 潛規則

非對稱格子（一奇一偶）：角點數必須一奇一偶，例如 $9 \times 6$ 或 $7 \times 8$，絕不能用 $8 \times 8$。對稱板具有 $180°$ 旋轉對稱性，偵測器無法分辨方向，導致 $R$ 矩陣的座標軸隨機翻轉。

物體空間座標系（傳入 calibrateCamera 的三維座標）：

  • 原點 $(0,0,0)$ — 左上角第一個內角點（本示例中紅色圓點處）。
  • $X$ 軸（紅色箭頭） — 向右，沿欄方向增加（長邊）。
  • $Y$ 軸（綠色箭頭） — 向下，沿列方向增加（短邊）。
  • $Z$ 軸 — 依右手定則，指向板面背後（遠離相機）。

行優先掃描順序：物體點清單先走 $X$（欄），再走 $Y$（列），與偵測器輸出順序一致：

$$\mathrm{idx} = r \cdot \mathrm{cols} + c, \qquad \mathbf{X}_{rc} = (c \cdot s,\; r \cdot s,\; 0)$$

其中 $s$ 為棋盤格的真實物理邊長（建議使用公尺）。

實務重點 — (1) $s$ 使用真實物理單位，解出的平移向量 $t$ 才有距離意義（$t_z = 0.5$ 代表相機距板面 50 cm）。 (2) 棋盤格四周必須留至少一格白邊（quiet zone），否則邊緣角點因缺乏對比度而偵測失敗。 (3) 絕對不要用正方形格子（$N \times N$）：旋轉 $90°$ 會讓 $X$ 與 $Y$ 軸悄悄互換，導致 SLAM 或多相機系統的姿態估計不穩定。
